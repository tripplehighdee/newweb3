🎓 Study Permit

Study in Canada

Canada is home to world-class universities — UofT, McGill, UBC, and hundreds more. A Study Permit allows full-time study at Designated Learning Institutions (DLIs) with access to a Post-Graduate Work Permit (PGWP) upon graduation.

Requirements & Criteria
  • Valid acceptance letter from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI)
  • Proof of financial support — min. CAD $10,000/year living expenses + tuition
  • Valid Nigerian passport (6+ months beyond your planned study period)
  • IELTS Academic — typically 6.0 overall (institution-specific requirements apply)
  • Academic transcripts: WAEC/NECO, O-Levels, university certificates (if applicable)
  • Compelling Statement of Purpose / Study plan
  • Proof of ties to Nigeria — family, property, employment
  • Biometrics enrollment at a Visa Application Centre (VAC)
  • Medical examination by IRCC-designated physician
  • Police clearance / criminal record certificate

✈️ Temporary Resident Visa

Visit Canada

Explore Canada's stunning landscapes, vibrant cities, or visit family with a Temporary Resident Visa (TRV). We prepare compelling applications that demonstrate clear intent to return, maximizing approval chances.

Requirements & Criteria
  • Valid Nigerian passport with minimum 6 months validity beyond travel dates
  • Completed application forms (IMM 5257 + Schedule 1)
  • Recent passport photographs (per Canadian specifications)
  • Bank statements (6–12 months) showing sufficient funds
  • Pay slips / employment letter / business registration documents
  • Detailed travel itinerary — flight reservation, hotel bookings
  • Invitation letter from Canadian host (if visiting family/friends)
  • Travel insurance for the full duration of stay
  • Strong proof of ties to Nigeria: property, employment, family
  • Biometrics enrollment at a Visa Application Centre

💼 Work Permit

Work in Canada

Canada actively recruits skilled workers across healthcare, tech, trades, agriculture, and more. From employer-specific permits to open work permits, we match your profile to the right pathway.

Requirements & Criteria
  • Valid job offer from a Canadian employer (for employer-specific permits)
  • Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) from the Canadian employer
  • Relevant educational qualifications and professional certifications
  • Minimum 1–2 years of relevant work experience in the field
  • IELTS General Training — CLB 5 or higher
  • Valid passport and all supporting identification documents
  • Proof of financial stability during the application period
  • Police clearance / criminal record certificate
  • Medical exam clearance (required for certain occupations)
  • Professional credential evaluation (ECA) where applicable

🏡 Permanent Residency

Settle in Canada

PR gives you the right to live, work, and study anywhere in Canada. Multiple streams exist — Express Entry, PNPs, Family Sponsorship. We identify your optimal pathway and build the strongest possible profile.

Requirements & Criteria
  • Express Entry CRS score (varies by draw — typically 470+ for FSW)
  • Minimum 1 year skilled work experience (NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3)
  • Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) for foreign degrees
  • IELTS CLB 7+ (or equivalent French test — TEF Canada / TCF Canada)
  • Proof of sufficient settlement funds (based on family size per IRCC table)
  • Clean police clearance from Nigeria and all countries lived in for 6+ months
  • Medical examination by an IRCC-designated physician
  • Provincial Nomination Certificate if applying via a PNP stream
  • Valid passport for the entire application processing period
  • Employment reference letters verifying duties, salary, and hours worked

🍁 Canadian Citizenship

Become a Canadian Citizen

After holding PR, you may be eligible for full Canadian citizenship. We advise on eligibility, physical presence calculations, application preparation, and citizenship test preparation with confidence.

Requirements & Criteria
  • Must hold Canadian Permanent Residency status
  • Physically present in Canada for 3 of the last 5 years (1,095 days minimum)
  • Canadian taxes filed (if required) for at least 3 of the last 5 years
  • Minimum age of 18 (minors may be included in a parent's application)
  • Pass the Canadian citizenship knowledge test (history, rights, responsibilities)
  • Demonstrate adequate knowledge of English or French
  • Intention to reside in Canada or serve in the Canadian Armed Forces
  • No criminal prohibitions or conditions under the Immigration Act
